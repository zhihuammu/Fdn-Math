Exercise 7.5

\(f(x)\) is an odd function, when \(x>0\), \(f(x)=\log_2 x\); when \(x<0\), \(f(x)\) should be :

  1. \(-\log_2 x\)

  2. \(\log_2 (-x)\)

  3. \(\log_2 x\)

  4. \(-\log_2 (-x)\)

Solution to Exercise 7.5

When \(x>0\), we have \(x=|x|\) and \(f(x)=f(|x|)=\log_2 |x|\).

Since \(f(x)\) is odd, so we have \(f(-|x|)=-f(|x|)\) when \(x>0\).

Therefore, when \(x<0\) we should have

\[f(x)=-f(|x|) = -\log_2 |x| = -\log_2 (-x) \]

Exercise 7.6

The real solutions of quadratic equation \(2(m+1)x^2+4mx+3m-2=0\) have the same sign, then \(m\) should satisfy:

  1. \(-2<m<-1\)

  2. \(-2\leq m < -1 \) or \( \frac{2}{3} < m \leq 1\)

  3. \(m<-1\) or \( m> \frac{2}{3}\)

  4. \(-2<m<-1\) or \( \frac{2}{3}<m \leq 1\)

Solution to Exercise 7.6

The quadratic equation has real solutions, so its discriminator should be larger than or equal to zero

\[\begin{align*} \Delta & = b^2-4ac \\ & = (4m)^2 - 4\left[2(m+1)\right](3m-2) \\ & = 16m^2 - 8(m+1)(3m-2) \\ & = 16m^2 - 8(3m^2+m-2) \\ & = 8\left[ 2m^2 -(3m^2+m-2)\right] \\ & = 8(-m^2-m+2) \\ & = -8(m^2+m-2) \\ & = -8(m+2)(m-1) \end{align*}\]

\(\Delta \geq 0\), so \( -8(m+2)(m-1)\geq 0 \). Therefore we obtain

\[-2 \leq m \leq 1 \]

The product of the two solutions is given by

\[x_1 x_2 = \frac{c}{a} = \frac{3m-2}{2(m+1)} \]

The two solutions of the equation have the same sign, so

\[\frac{3m-2}{2(m+1)} \geq 0 \]

Therefore we can obtain \(m<-1\) or \(m\geq \frac{2}{3}\)

In conclusion, \(m\) should satisfy the condition

\[-2\leq m<-1 \]

or

\[\frac{2}{3}\leq m \leq 1 \]

\(f(x)\) is odd function and \(f(x+2)=f(x)\), when \(0<x<1\), \(f(x)=\log_2 x\). Supposing \(\displaystyle a=f\left(\frac{6}{5}\right)\), \(\displaystyle b=f\left(\frac{3}{2}\right)\), \(\displaystyle c=f\left(\frac{5}{2}\right)\), then they have the following relationship:

  1. \(a<b<c\)

  2. \(b<a<c\)

  3. \(c<b<a\)

  4. \(c<a<b\)

Solution to Exercise 7.7

When \(0<x<1\), \(f(x)=\log_2 x\) and it is an odd function, so we have the following graph

where \(f(-x)=-f(x)\) for \(-1\leq x <0 \) and \( 0< x \leq 1\).

We also know that the function is periodic, and its period is 2. So we can obtain

\[\begin{align*} & a = f\left( \frac{6}{5}\right)= f\left( 2- \frac{4}{5}\right) = f\left( -\frac{4}{5}\right)= -f\left( \frac{4}{5}\right) \\ & b = f\left( \frac{3}{2}\right)= f\left( 2- \frac{1}{2}\right) = f\left( -\frac{1}{2}\right)= -f\left( \frac{1}{2}\right) \\ & c = f\left( \frac{5}{2}\right)= f\left( 2+ \frac{1}{2}\right) = f\left( \frac{1}{2}\right)= f\left( \frac{1}{2}\right) \end{align*}\]

Marking these points on the graph, we will know that \underline{\(c < a < b\)}.

Exercise 7.8

Supposing \(0<x<y<a<1\), which of the followings is correct?

  1. \(\log_a (xy)<0\)

  2. \(0<\log_a(xy)<1\)

  3. \(1<\log_a(xy)\)

  4. \(\log_a(xy)>2\)

Solution to Exercise 7.8

Assuming \(a^m = x\) and \(a^n=y\), we could obtain that

\[m>1, \quad n>1 \]

So

\[\begin{align*} & \log_a x = m > 1 \\ & \log_a y = n > 1 \end{align*}\]

Therefore \(\log_a x + \log_a y = \log_a (xy) = m+n>2 \)

Exercise 7.9

Supposing \(f(x)=\log\frac{2+x}{2-x}\), the domain of the \( g(x)= f\left(\frac{x}{2}\right)+f\left(\frac{2}{x}\right)\) should be \underline{\hspace{1cm}}.

  1. \((-4, 0) \cup (0, 4)\)

  2. \((-4, -1) \cup (1, 4)\)

  3. \((-2, -1) \cup (1, 2)\)

  4. \((-4, -2) \cup (2, 4)\)

Solution to Exercise 7.9

\[\begin{align*} g(x) & = f(\frac{x}{2}) + f(\frac{2}{x}) \\ & = \log \frac{2+\frac{x}{2}}{2- \frac{x}{2}} + \log \frac{2+\frac{2}{x}}{2-\frac{2}{x}} \\ & = \log \frac{4+x}{4-x} + \log \frac{x+1}{x-1} \qquad(x\neq 0) \\ \end{align*}\]

For a logarithm function \(\log X\), \(X\) should be larger than zero, therefore we should have

\[\begin{split} \left\{ \begin{aligned} & \frac{4+x}{4-x} > 0 \qquad(1)\\ & \frac{x+1}{x-1}> 0 \qquad(2) \end{aligned} \right . \end{split}\]

From condition (1), we obtain \(x\in (-4, 4)\). From condition (2), we obtain \(x\in (-\infty, -1) \cup (1,+\infty)\). \(x\) should satisfy both conditions, therefore we obtain

\[x \in (-4, -1) \cup (1, 4) \]

Exercise 7.10

\[\begin{split} f(x) = \left\{ \begin{array}{ll} (3a-1)x+4a, & x<1 \\ \log_a x, & x\geq 1 \end{array} \right. \end{split}\]

is a decreasing function, the range of \(a\) is:

  1. \(0<a<1\)

  2. \(\displaystyle 0<a<\frac{1}{3}\)

  3. \(\displaystyle \frac{1}{7}\leq a < \frac{1}{3}\)

  4. \(\displaystyle \frac{1}{7} \leq a < 1\)

Solution to Exercise 7.10

\(f(x)\) is a decreasing function, therefore we can know that

  1. \((3a-1)x+4a\) is decreasing for \(x<1\)

  2. \(\log_a x\) is decreasing for \(x\ge 1\)

  3. \(\left.\left[ (3a-1)x+4a\right]\right|_{x=1} \ge \left. \log_a x \right|_{x=1}\)

From the first condition, we know that the function is a straight line for \(x<1\) and it should have a negative gradient, therefore

\[3a-1 <0 \Rightarrow a<\frac{1}{3} \]

From the second condition, we know that \(a\) should be a positive number less than 1, therefore

\[0<a<1 \]

From the third condition, we obtain

\[(3a-1)+4a \ge \log_a 1 \]
\[\Rightarrow 7a-1 \ge 0 \]
\[a \ge \frac{1}{7} \]

All the three conditions should be satisfied, therefore

\[\frac{1}{7} \le a < \frac{1}{3} \]

Exercise 7.11

The domain of the function \(y=\log_a(kx^2+4kx+3)\) is \(\mathbb{R}\), find the ranges of \(k\).

Solution to Exercise 7.11

For a general logarithm function \(f(x)=\log_a x\), \(x\) should be larger than zero. So for \(y=\log_a (kx^2+4kx+3)\), we can obtain

\[kx^2+4kx+3>0 \]

for \(x\in \mathbf{R}\). We need to find the minimum value of \(g(x)=kx^2+4kx+3\) and ensure it is larger than zero

\[\begin{align*} g(x)= & kx^2+4kx+3 \\ = & kx^2+4kx+4k-4k+3 \\ = & k(x^2+4x+4)-4k+3 \\ = & k(x+2)^2 +3-4k \end{align*}\]

When \(x=-2\), the minimum of \(g(x)\) is \(3-4k\) and it should be larger than zero, so

\[3-4k>0 \]

therefore

\[k< \frac{3}{4} \]

Exercise 7.12

Function \(f(x)=2ax+2a+1\), \(x \in [-1, 1]\) has both positive and negative values, find the ranges of \(a\).

Solution to Exercise 7.12

This is a straight line segment, at the two ends the function has the two extrema (maximum and minimum).

\[\begin{align*} & f(-1)=2a(-1)+2a+1=1 > 0 \\ & f(1)=2a(1)+2a+1=4a+1 \end{align*}\]

As the function also has negative values, we can obtain

\[f(1)=4a+1< 0 \]
\[\Rightarrow a < -\frac{1}{4} \]

Exercise 7.13

The brightness of light reduces by \(10\%\) when the light passes through a piece of glass of 1 cm thickness. How many pieces of glasses with the same thickness would be needed to reduce the brightness of light by \(\frac{2}{3}\).

Solution to Exercise 7.13

Without any glass, the brightness is 100%. After one piece of glass, the brightness becomes \(100\% \times 0.9^1\). After \(x\) pieces of glass, the brightness is

\[b(x) = 100\% \times 0.9^x \]

Reducing the brightness by \(\frac{2}{3}\), we can obtain

\[100\% \times 0.9^x = \frac{1}{3} \]

Therefore

\[x= \log_{0.9} \frac{1}{3} = 10.43 \]

Thus we need 11 pieces of glasses.

Exercise 7.14

Supposing \(a>0\), \(\displaystyle f(x)= \frac{e^x}{a}+\frac{a}{e^x}\) is an even function.

  1. Find the value of \(a\);

  2. Prove that \(f(x)\) is an increasing function for \(x\in (0, +\infty)\).

Solution to Exercise 7.14

  1. \[f(x)= \frac{e^x}{a}+\frac{a}{e^x} = \frac{1}{a} e^x + a e^{-x} \]
    \[f(-x)= \frac{e^{-x}}{a}+\frac{a}{e^{-x}} = \frac{1}{a} e^{-x} + a e^x \]

    \(f(x)\) is even, so \(f(-x)=f(x)\), we can obtain

    \[\frac{1}{a} e^x + a e^{-x} = \frac{1}{a} e^{-x} + a e^x \]
    \[\Rightarrow \left(\frac{1}{a}-a\right)e^x + \left(a-\frac{1}{a}\right)e^{-x} = 0 \]

    Since \(e^x > 0\) and \(e^{-x}>0\) for \(x\in R\), we can obtain

    \[\frac{1}{a}-a =0 \]
    \[\Rightarrow a=\pm 1 \]

    \(a>0\), so \(a=1\).

  2. Idea: \(x\in (0, +\infty)\), for \(u>0\) if we could prove \(\Delta = f(x+u) - f(x)>0\), then \(f(x)\) is an increasing function.

    \[\begin{align*} \Delta & = f(x+u) - f(x) \\ & = \left ( e^{x+u} + e^{-x-u}\right) - \left( e^x + e^{-x} \right) \\ & = \left ( e^{x+u} - e^{x}\right) + \left( e^{-x-u} - e^{-x} \right) \\ & = e^x \left(e^u-1\right) + e^{-x} ( e^{-u} -1) \\ & = e^x \left(e^u-1\right) - e^{-x} ( 1-e^{-u} ) \\ & = e^{-x}\left(1-e^{-u}\right) \left[ \frac{e^x(e^u-1)}{e^{-x}(1-e^{-u})} - 1 \right] \\ & = e^{-x}\left(1-e^{-u}\right) \left[ \frac{e^{2x}(e^u-1)}{(1-e^{-u})} - 1 \right] \\ & = e^{-x}\left(1-e^{-u}\right) \left[ \frac{e^{2x}e^u(e^u-1)}{e^u(1-e^{-u})} - 1 \right] \\ & = e^{-x}\left(1-e^{-u}\right) \left[ \frac{e^{2x}e^u(e^u-1)}{e^u-1} - 1 \right] \\ & = e^{-x}\left(1-e^{-u}\right) \left( e^{2x}e^u - 1 \right) \\ \end{align*}\]

    For \(x>0\) and \(u>0\), we know

    • \(e^x>1\), \(e^{2x}>1\) and \(e^u>1\) \(\quad \Rightarrow e^{2x}e^u - 1> 0\)

    • \(0<e^{-x}<0\)

    • \(0<e^{-u}<1\) \(\quad \Rightarrow 0<1-e^{-u}<1 \)

    Thus \(e^{-x}\left(1-e^{-u}\right) \left( e^{2x}e^u - 1 \right)>0\), and we have proved \(f(x)\) is an increasing function for \(x\in (0, +\infty)\).

Exercise 7.15

Supposing the domain of the function \(\displaystyle f(x) = \sqrt{2-\frac{x+3}{x-1}}\) is \(\mathbf{A}\), the domain of the function \(g(x) = \log \left[(x-a-1)(2a-x)\right]\), \(a<1\) is \(\mathbf{B}\).

  1. Find \(\mathbf{A}\);

  2. If \(\mathbf{B} \subseteq \mathbf{A}\), find the ranges of \(a\).

Solution to Exercise 7.15

  1. Under the square bracket, the value should satisfy two conditions

    • \(\displaystyle 2-\frac{x+3}{x-1} \ge 0\)

    • \(x-1\ne 0\)

    From the first condition, we obtain

    \[\begin{align*} & 2-\frac{x+3}{x-1} \ge 0 \\ \Rightarrow & \frac{x-5}{x-1} \ge 0 \\ \Rightarrow & x\ge 5 ~~ \mathrm{or}~~ x \le 1 \end{align*}\]

    From the second condition, we obtain

    \[x \ne 1 \]

    Thus \(x\ge 5\) or \(x < 1\), therefore \(\mathbf{A}=(-\infty, 1) \cup [5,+\infty)\)

  2. For \(g(x)=\log \left[(x-a-1)(2a-x)\right]\), \(a<1\), we should have

    \[\quad (x-a-1)(2a-x)>0 \]
    \[\Rightarrow (x-a-1)(x-2a)<0 \]
    \[\Rightarrow 2a < x < a+1 \]
    \[\mathbf{B}=(2a, a+1) \]

    Note: \(a<1\), ~~\(2a<a+1\)

    As \(\mathbf{B} \subseteq \mathbf{A}\), we could have

    \[a+1 \le 1 \Rightarrow a\le 0 \]

    or

    \[2a \ge 5 \Rightarrow a\ge \frac{5}{2} \]

    Because \(a<1\), so we should have \(a\le 0\) to ensure \(\mathbf{B}\) is a subset of \(\mathbf{A}\).
